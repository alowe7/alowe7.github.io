I'm an old computer guy

Created At：2026-02-17 17:36 Updated At：2026-02-27 16:08
Keywords: journal

I'm an old computer guy.  when I say old, I mean old.

I learned to program using punched cards, ok?

before that, I had a job that involved mounting certain magnetic tapes on those old vacuum tapie-wheelie drives, and heave a shoebox full of punched cards that some other motherfucker wrote into a card reader, and the program made the tapes whirr and spin and do god knows what, eventually pooping out a printout.  when the programmers came by to pick their output, they often cursed and went back to the drawing board.  sometimes they were pleased with the output.

one time I asked one of the guys who wrote the programs what was going on, and he kind of liked me and said "you should go into programming."  that was 1975, before I went to UT and eventually went into computer science.

If I knew then what I know now, huh?

but I did know some thing back then, maybe some things I've forgotten now, to be honest.

I'm not a completely different human being.  it's kind of a spectrum, getting from there to here.  not a discontinuity.  some things lost and some things gained.

but before that I had touched a computer, or more specifically, a teletype machine, the old kind with the spool of thermal paper coming out the back, and the loud clickety-clack ibm selectric typewriter feel, but what was I doing?  playing that old text-oriented game of star trek.  it was kind of a version of "battleships" in space, in 3d, in your mind at least, with photon torpedoes and phasers and all that stuff.  it was fun.

I was originally interested in computers as an art medium.  really.  that was the way I thought back then.  I thought I was an artist.

I did some stuff, most of which is lost.  maybe properly so. from this perspective, I'm not ok with my work from back then.  sometimes it seems like the product of a diseased mind.

but anyway, the idea was to use computer graphics as a way to compensate for my poor technique as a visual artist, with paint, pencils and pastels, and stuff like that.

I was, and am, really terrible at it.  but I was motivated, anyway.  so that's good, right?

so that seems like an unlikely path.  but it turns out I'm also pretty good at math, so it turns out I could make computers do things.  so that was cool.

I remember a guy.  he was a colleague back in the day.  but super smart.  a better man than me.  Eric Schoen.

one time he said to me, "we are the luckiest mathematicians who ever lived.  we get to see our equations get up and dance, and have effect in the world."

haha.  God bless him.

that was many years later, but still many years ago.  and I confess, quite motivating.

so there's this concept of "deep vs. wide"  and at first I went deep.  I specialized in computer graphics.  I know a fair amount about it, as of say, 1990.

I wasn't even that deep into it yet, but that well went dry, so to speak, at an inconvenient point in my career, with a house and a wife and a young family.

so then I decided to go wide, as a strategy, to make a career, and be a generalist.

and that worked out ok, I guess.  could have been better, but could have been a lot worse.

but the point of all this is, I don't care about any of that shit anymore, and maybe its time to turn around and go back and recover those lost values, and you know, whatever.  :-)

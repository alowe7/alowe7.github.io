nun komm, der heiden heiland

now come, Savior of the gentiles,

so I was sitting on my deck.
its February in Texas, changeable, but pretty nice today
I swam this morning as the sun rose over my shoulder
I prayed and thanked God.

I thanked God for this body that is still able to do this
and I'm getting stronger.
It's a weird disease where the only therapy known to at least slow down the inevitable degeneration is exercise
and exercise has other effects, benefits or changes of various kinds.

at least for now.  combined with the aging process, its well, new ground.

sometimes I don't even want to write it down.

I mean, God are you out there?  what is going on?

Well thanks for these gifts in any case, even if it is only for now.

Religiosity seems like a fool's game, until you find yourself lost at sea with nothing but this rock to cling to.

[cf. In the smothering blackness of night]

 

I'm sleeping a lot lately.
I often go to bed with the sun, and rise likewise.
it often amounts to 10 or 12 hours a night.

it feels natural somehow.  and I learned a long time ago to listen to my body.  its much wiser than I am about these things.

why do I do it?  because I can!

I do wake up several times in the night, like a lot of guys my age, but I don't have any problem getting back to sleep.

also, I don't have to get up at any particular time, so that makes the waking process a little less, shall we say, abrupt?

one consequence of this, is I have more "surface area" between sleeping and waking.

anyway, I am starting to think about my dreams differently.

so I'm sitting on the deck and I recalled a dream I had last night, or rather early this morning.

Casper had run away
we were back at Lonsdale
and Anita blamed me for it
"you left the gate open again"
and I grew angry with her, in part because of course she was right.
I remember the anger, but in my dream it was disproportionate
I don't remember the details now.
I do remember noticing one of the cushions sitting there in the yard
with the stuffing coming out of it
and I knew that was something Casper would have done
and then he was there. he was back.

that's the dream. but then my mind started to wander
and I thought about Dr. Chudzik and how he's interested in my dreams
and I started rehearsing what I would say.
maybe recalling this dream or some others
I've got a million of them.

so I began preparing my script and rehearsing my lines
and my mind began to wander again and I remember the question he once asked me:
what are your hopes for yourself physically, emotionally and spiritually?
I think he was picking up on something I had said,
and I had answers for the first two, that I think are satisfactory and within reason.
but I got tripped up on the last one. not because I didn't have an answer,
there may be more than one, but I definitely have too many.
also there's the problem of vocabulary, and grounded concepts
I mean you can get there. sometimes. but it takes a while just to set the framing.

and then my mind began to wander again, and I realized there was a swallow in my crepe myrtle
fluffing his feathers. and I think there was a girl swallow in the live oak he had his eye on.
and I thought of a conversation I had with Liz last night.
I feel like she and Gareth are in a good place. new baby. all that.
its natural.  and nature is hopeful, optimistic.

so what does spiritual well being look like?

I thought I knew, but I had to think about that one.

it depends on what you believe, how you frame your existence
but what you're usually looking for are answers to the problem of evil and the question of death.

[cf. What do I believe?]

I don't have them, nobody does. and if someone tells you they do, they're either lying to you or to themselves or both.

most of the rest can take care of itself.
there may be one more to add to the list: I call it the problem of temptation.

like, why did God make us this way if he's only going to punish us for following our nature?  the nature which He gave us?   Its a puzzler.

another way to frame this same thing is in terms of the tension and apparent conflict between physical reality and different views one may have on spiritual reality.

you can pick any number of examples. but to me some parts of it sound more like just establishing societal norms than bona fide commandments from the deity.

I mean, that's ok.  but it's not the same.

then I thought of how I've started working on memorizing things, for some reason, mental exercise, I guess.
and how I'm kind of on the lookout for good things to work on.

like the ten commandments, can you name them?   in any one of their several variations and divisions?

And even if you can name them, do you follow them?

or maybe per MLK: "there are spiritual laws in the universe!  as real and as inexorable as gravity!  and you ignore them at your own peril!"

and job 38 came to mind and that epic speech from God Himself:

“Who is this who darkens counsel
By words without knowledge?
Now prepare yourself like a man;
I will question you, and you shall answer Me.
“Where were you when I laid the foundations of the earth?
Tell Me, if you have understanding.
Who determined its measurements?
Surely you know!
Or who stretched the line upon it?
To what were its foundations fastened?
Or who laid its cornerstone,
When the morning stars sang together,
And all the sons of God shouted for joy?

then I thought of another journal entry

[cf. judgment day]

and terry tao. probably the greatest living mathematician, and when asked to give a lecture, he chooses as a topic the cosmic distance ladder, and how when you think about that, I mean the fact of it, as well as why he might have chosen that topic, you can't help but think that God made us this way for a reason.  (seriously, look it up.)

so its mixed together: some of the rules we get from our religions are important and meaningful, eternally and at a cosmic level, and some are perhaps important societal norms. I mean that's valid.  and necessary.  there's more than one way to successfully organize a society, and any number of ways for societies to fail. and some of these things I'm talking about may be variable across societies and eras, without compromising the integrity of the society, but some other aspects may be constant.  everything evolves and decays and regenerates.  or not.

and then I thought of ibn khaldun, and islam

and I returned to my script, wherein I would demonstrate to dr. chudzik how smart I am by pronouncing on the relationship and the differences between islam and christianity. like one of those old kid's toy dolls with the pull string in the back. pull the string and out come my rehearsed lines. I am a robot.

or am I? I guess this projection of myself in my mind right now is some sort of automaton, but that is not me in reality, now or then. that is just a concept.

islam means surrender. and I am on board with that part of islam. surrender to the will of God. not only is it right and just that you should do this, it is ironically liberating. this may entail another deep question then, the impenetrable conundrum of free will vs. determinism, which is bound up with the question of time. what. the. fuck. is. going. on. with. time?!

not for nothing, but there's lot's about islam that I am not on board with. maybe they had some big hits way back in one of those "caliphates of the golden age" days, but their approach to scholarship or indeed any discussion about their own origins is anathema or even subject to fatwah.

or you might say they have their own scholastic traditions, but I mean come on. europe went through this centuries ago with christianity. it was bloody. but we've moved past that. haven't we?

what surprised me when I looked into it, is how much the koran and thus islam owe to the prior christian and judaic traditions.  there's no shame in it.  the stories overlap.  partially.  and diverge.  anyone can see it, without invalidating either tradition, can't they?  so let's start with what is shared and what maybe in some sense might be a survivability trait of these different but related belief systems.

then I started thinking about an entirely different essay topic: Paul and the Prophet, comparing and contrasting the apostle Paul and the Prophet Mohammed in a historical context.

where they all seem to go wrong is the same too.  there's something about the power of priesthood, its intoxicating, absolute.  And everything is ok for a while, but after a couple of generations they always wind up in some kind of sorcerer's apprentice situation, where they claim they can read the horoscopes, or whatever, and guide the king let's say into a battle which may have been won or lost by any number of other factors.  or maybe your temple gets destroyed and your precious idol or whatever gets stolen, or destroyed.  and then what?

as long as you can keep getting it right, being a prognosticator to the king can be a pretty good gig.  but it always goes sideways in the end.  the worst part is they never go ahead and die.  something about these ideas contain within themselves their own perpetuity.  religions serve a purpose, I guess.  but the hypocrisy.  its just too much.  anyway, whatever that is, its not spirituality.

 

so what is spirituality, then? is it that powers and beings beyond our understanding are present and direct or otherwise influence our lives and the flow of the world? maybe angels, or demons, or something else?  the spirits of our ancestors, or maybe even generations yet unborn looking back at us through time from the future? maybe our own future selves?

maybe.  but I doubt it.  and even if it does exist, if it has no effect, it might as well not.   haha.

maybe externalized projections of our own unconscious?  ok, maybe sometimes.  but not always, according to nothing but just my gut.

 

spirit means breath. in the sense of the essence of life. spirituality then might be framed around that, but what I have in mind encompasses that, but is is a little broader. thus, the issue of vocabulary.

 

so what I mean by spirituality is all of that.

ok.  then what does spiritual well-being look like?

and I recalled that my immediate response to dr. chudzik's question was that I continue to look forward through life, with hope and courage, right up to the very end, and have a peaceful transition into the next life, and that I will be welcomed into the arms of my Lord when that fateful day comes.

And I'm not ashamed to say that I pray that those I love share a like destiny, that God protect us and have mercy on us all.

What I mean is that my - our - spiritual well-being is not our own, but remains subject to God's will.  I don't know.  but I hope.  and I have a feeling.

What we can do is open our hearts to that.   that's what I'm talking about.

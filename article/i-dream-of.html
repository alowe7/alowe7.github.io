I dream of a land where a search for
“Night of the broken records”

Returns something meaningful, perhaps depending on location connectivity and context, only for me,

Listening to wah-wah freshly possibly for the first time in a long time, and maybe it was the bose 301s vs the whatever I have going downstairs, they may be before bose even had a coherent numbering scheme, I don’t know

But the thing I wanted to capture was this comparison of wah-wah as produced in the original lp version on George Harrison’s all things must pass album which was in some ways a watershed event not only from the audience or fan perspective but also for the players who were going through some tough years maybe sometimes self inflicted but the drug situation was not good and sometimes killed people but always fucked them up one way or another

What I mean is the metaphorical juxtaposition of Phil Spector mix as representing my original experience which my father tried to destroy that night but he failed

And years later I bought the cd version and I thought "this is different, somehow"

Haha

But it turned out Harrison remixed the cd version and any digital version you are likely to hear will also be that mix

But Spector's version was just this wall of sound

And then years later I was in half price books and found a copy of the old lp and I was all over that and other records besides that I had lost that dark and fateful night

They were precious to me. I had no money.  these were my own few special things.

but he destroyed them carelessly in a fit of his own madness.  it hurt me, but it really had nothing to do with me.  he was just all about his own world.

But then in my dream I imagined my mother intervening in heaven and saying let’s not let anger define us because being in heaven she understood both me and my father in a way she didn’t before

And to be honest we understood each other better

I mean I only had one father but he had a wife and four sons and all their stuff to deal with to the extent he even thought of us or anyone other than himself

not to mention his own father who turns out to be a real cipher.

But never mind that

And another part of that was that in my dream he admitted he had done wrong but also I didn’t appreciate the things he did do for me and would do even in the future of that moment

Which was true.

And I admitted that that was something I didn’t appreciate until I became a father myself and now a grandfather so I see things a little differently and maybe he does too

The bottom line is we just forgave each other and agreed to move on

And yes it did feel like something that happened in the present tense

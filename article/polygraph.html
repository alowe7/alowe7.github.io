journal about getting polygraphed.

I think I've been polygraphed twice.

the first time especially as I recall was pretty stressful.

I already had ts-sci clearance, with a number of compartments mostly having to do with my work for the army.

I'm not ashamed to say we did some good work with the army during the second gulf war.   We can sometimes get shit done when we put our minds to it.

but this new project was intelligence community, administered by the fbi, who were pretty heavy-handed not to mention shitty project managers.
Just imagine a bunch of cops doing high tech acquisition. Barney Miller meets mission impossible.  with some house of cards thrown in, just for fun.

Its a little bit comical, or maybe a little sad, when you think about it. I inherited this whole situation and so my feelings about it, you might say, are inflected.

the high tech government acquisition process is pretty bad all over.  it certainly was in my day.

I actually know a lot about that.

its probably bad in new and different ways now.

but the point here is the ic doesn't respect dod issued ts clearance or grant sci access to anyone without a polygraph. the dod will do this in most cases for compartments they control.

so it does make you scratch your head, why they do it, especially since polygraphs are pretty much discredited.

but my guess is while they may not be definitive, they're not totally useless especially if you add some humint interpretive layers over the top.

and the whole process gives them a reason to reject you without telling you why or just saying something vague like, "your polygraph was inconclusive."

turns out no one ever fails a polygraph.  they just sometimes come back inconclusive. which is their way of saying "fuck you"

But anyway there might be a good reason to want to deny someone access without having to say why.

not in the usual case, like you or me or anybody, but if you do get a bad guy and maybe you can't say why you know he's a bad guy, without potentially compromising sources and methods.

in my field what you'd worry about might more likely be someone who’s possibly vulnerable to influence, more of a victim or maybe even just a freethinker, not really a bad guy per se.

but anyway, still a risk.

that's not to say the system can't be abused. abuse of the system is kind of a given. that's what this whole thing is all about.

people lie all the time.  don't you know that?

so the process is pretty formal, and you get contacted by specialists in this area and its pretty businesslike they take you to this nondescript place and introduce you to this nondescript person

And you’ve already been fingerprinted and bio scanned and everything.

One time they took our saliva they said for dna testing. No reason given why that was necessary.

It probably wasn’t. who knows? maybe they were just fucking around just to see how we'd react.

the psycho motherfuckers.

and you've got nothing to hide, but your livelihood is on the line.  I mean if this guy pulls some vague bullshit out of his ass, he can ruin your career.  not just this job, which you will definitely lose, but that would disqualify you from any job in this sector.

and maybe you'll never know why.

and there's even a double jeopardy situation where if you already had ts-sci from some other authority, say the dod, and the ic fucks you up the ass, maybe for no reason, they just don't like you, or maybe you think you got away with something up to that point, you then lose the clearances and accesses you did have.  I've seen it happen.

So a lot of people won’t subject themselves to that. Practically my whole team as it turns out. Most of whom were already dod cleared, and knew all of this and said "no thanks."

Except one guy who said "ok" and he got the inconclusive result treatment. So we had to give him the boot after that. One of my best guys. It was ridiculous I’m telling you. But then on the other hand, I'm thinking maybe they know something I don't.  or maybe they were just racist.

And I’m here with all the other shit I’ve got going on in my life and somehow I find myself trying to run a multi-million dollar top secret program with all the politics of that, I can't even tell you, and this clown circus team I had to deal with on my side and then Covid hit. I mean wow.

There’s a couple of different kinds of poly, at least that I know of. There’s what’s called a counter-intelligence poly. That's supposed to be pretty limited in scope. They ask you questions that relate to political affiliation, foreign contacts, "are you a commie?" or other stuff like that.

There’s what’s called a lifestyle poly where they ask you about drug use, infidelity, gambling, finances, stuff like that.  Whatever you might think, they don't care about your character, really.  Or maybe they do, some of these people are ridiculous.  But really, the idea is to find out if you can be manipulated somehow.

And there’s what’s called a full scope poly where they ask you about all of that and anything else they please. Sometimes not in a nice way, confrontational even. That’s the treatment I got.

and they wire you up, head to ass in sensors and with a strap across your forehead and another one across your chest and a whoopee cushion under your ass and they make you face a wall and they ask you questions, and challenge you in what is maybe for you a new and different way.

And you’re sitting there wondering how in the world you wound up here, and who you even are anymore.

not fun.

but you do it anyway.  why?  because of the dollars.  its all about the dollars.

At one point he asked me “have you ever lied to anyone?”  Which I thought was a bullshit question.
And I said “no” which we both knew was the bullshit answer it deserved.
And he said “I’m getting an inconclusive result”
And I said, “well, then, yes, I guess. I thought you meant in a professional context. once my wife was terminal with cancer and she asked me if I thought she was going to make it and I lied and I told her I did.”

And I imagined his needles went off the fucking chart at that point.

He said he thought I was being evasive.

I didn't tell him everything I thought, but they did let me get on with my work, and that's all I cared about.

judgment day

in most forms of Christianity there's this concept of judgment day.

and usually its framed as something terrifying, eternal, cosmic.

mostly it comes from what is sometimes called the book of revelation

and a childlike almost willful ignorance that surrounds it.

most people assume its supposed to come when you die, but there's one big one at the end of time for everybody.

or maybe both.  or neither.  who knows?

of course, its gibberish and nonsense, unless you decode it.  and maybe even then, still.

the most correct interpretation is that it is a sort of coded message hidden in an apparent fantasia

most of the references are coded because they were Christians living underground in roman society,

to decode it, you first need to understand that the Antichrist is the roman emperor Nero, and so on.

if you were in prison for whatever reason and you started badmouthing the emperor,

and a mad one at that, the madness of the emperor permeates the empire of which he is apotheosis.

you would find yourself in hot water for sure.  thus the need for coded messages.

so forget all that.

but do contemplate a sort of judgment day, when this life's toils and troubles are at an end

and you stand before God, once more in the light, in the presence of the Lord

and He inspects you, examines you, maybe like a judge, or maybe like an indulgent grandfather

maybe like an investigator, insightful, keen, but with compassion and a sense of humor

and maybe even the adversary accuses you.

and you can't even begin to defend yourself, but maybe you can find an advocate.   a witness.

can you?

 

I often imagine God speaks with the inverted intonation of an old Jewish man, humorously, but sagely.

I'll have to work on a for instance of that, maybe like that dream I had one time, when I could read God's words emblazoned across the sky in a heavenly script of golden fire.

and He had written: "I'm just saying."  And I just laughed.

 

but my point is, why not live your life as if that were going to happen.

maybe even in the anticipation lie the seeds of the thing itself.

but even if not, then why not?  what would you do differently?  why not do that now, anyway, then?

I have a lot of imaginings about that

where some wise guy atheist is up at bat, and he starts in with his sophistry and God silences him with a roar like a volcano or a tornado

and you hear the master's voice:

"Where were you when I laid the foundations of the earth?
Tell Me, if you have understanding.
Who fixed its measurements? Surely you know!
Or who stretched a measuring line across it?
On what were its foundations set,
or who laid its cornerstone
while the morning stars sang together
and all the sons of God shouted for joy?"

Haha. In the inimitable words of Keith Richards, "that's the shit!"

and then my imagining continues, when someone like Terry Tao is thus queried and responds with sincerity and in detail about the cosmic distance ladder, and just as importantly, how it was discerned, step by step.

and isn't it amazing what human beings can accomplish if we pay attention and use the gifts God has given us?

and I imagine God listening to him intently, dotingly, bemusedly.  giving him a pass, of course.

 

as for me, I don't have many answers, but I do have a clean conscience.

do I have regrets?  of course I do!

have I made mistakes?  of course I have.  many.

more than I'm even aware of and with more yet to come, no doubt.

maybe some things I regret, I shouldn't, and some things I don't regret but I should.

who knows?

but I will own my life.

and I will take my lumps.

 

God is merciful.  God is just.  God grants success.
